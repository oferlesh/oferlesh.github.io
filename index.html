About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the School of Computer Science and Engineering, Hebrew University of Jerusalem, advised by Prof. Orna Kupferman. I expect to complete my degree in October 2026. I completed both my B.Sc. in Mathematics and Computer Science (2019) and my M.Sc. in Computer Science (2021) at the Hebrew University, also under the supervision of Prof. Kupferman.

My research is driven by a fascination with clean, efficient representations of combinatorial objects. This perspective draws me to pure theoretical problems in automata theory, where states act as memory structures encoding history. I view different automata types as distinct data structures, each encoding information differently and thus naturally suited for distinct algorithmic tasks. Algorithmic problems on these objects often amount to manipulating a suitable data type or translating between automata types to cleanly capture the necessary information. I believe this perspective is key to tight algorithmic reasoning, even in domains beyond traditional automata theory.
